Did You Know....

Half of American families are living paycheck to paycheck

Source: marketwatch.com

Nearly 1 in 5 (19%) Americans have nothing set aside to cover an unexpected emergency, while nearly 1 in 3 (31%) Americans don’t have at least $500 set aside to cover an unexpected emergency expense, according to a survey released by HomeServe USA, a home repair service.

1 in 3 Americans have $0 saved for Retirement

Source: money.com

According to this article, due to various economic factors and not correctly prioritizing fianances, 33% of American have no money saved up for retirement with an only 23% of Americans having less than $10k saved

The average American consumer has a little more than $5,000 in credit card debt

Source: USAToday.com

According to Experian's latest State of Credit report, the average U.S. consumer holds about two bank-issued credit cards and carries a total balance of $5,551. That's a lot of money, especially if you're paying interest of 15% to 20%.

Americans hold more than $1 trillion in student debt

Source: marketwatch.com

Research indicates that the $1.2 trillion in student loan debt may be preventing Americans,from making the kinds of big purchases that drive economic growth, like house and cars, and reaching other milestones, such as having the ability to save for retirement

One in four student loan borrowers are either in delinquency or default on their student loans, according the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau.

About 40 million Americans hold student loans and about 70% of bachelor’s degree recipients graduate with debt.

Only 39% of Americans have enough savings to cover a $1,000 emergency

Source: cnbc.com

According to Bankrate’s latest financial security index survey, 34 percent of American households experienced a major unexpected expense over the past year. However, only 39 percent of survey respondents said they would be able to cover a $1,000 setback using their savings.

Using a credit card was the second most popular answer, with 19 percent of respondents saying they’d deal with an emergency with plastic and pay it off over time.
